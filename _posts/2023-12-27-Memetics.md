#### People don't have ideas. Ideas have people.

---

1. A **meme** is any information that can be copied by others in some way.
	The first point defines a "meme" as any information that can be copied by others in some way. In the context of this definition, it seems to extend beyond the colloquial use of the term "meme" to include any form of information that has the potential for replication. This broad definition encompasses not only internet memes but any piece of information that can be shared or reproduced by others. It raises questions about the nature and scope of information transmission, as well as the various mediums through which this replication can occur. Understanding the nuances of what constitutes "information" and the mechanisms of copying is essential for a comprehensive grasp of this definition.

2. Patterns of information that can indirectly influence their surroundings.
	The second point suggests that memes, as defined in the first point, are characterized by patterns of information that can indirectly influence their surroundings. This implies that the impact of memes extends beyond simple replication; they possess the ability to exert influence on the environment or context in which they exist.

3. This includes any text, thought, habit, action or symbol that can be understood or copied.
	  The third point expands the scope of what constitutes a meme by encompassing various forms of information. It includes not only textual elements but also thoughts, habits, actions, and symbols. This broad categorization highlights the versatility of memes, suggesting that they can manifest in diverse ways across different domains of human expression and behavior.


4. Examples of memes: ideas, stories, ideologies, jokes, narratives, brands, identities, habits, practices.
	The fourth point provides specific examples of memes, further illustrating the diverse range of information that falls under this concept. The examples listed include ideas, stories, ideologies, jokes, narratives, brands, identities, habits, and practices. Each of these examples represents a distinct category of information that can be transmitted, replicated, and influence its surroundings, as discussed in the previous points. This list showcases the breadth of memes, spanning from abstract concepts like ideologies and narratives to tangible elements like brands and habits. Understanding these examples helps to concretize the concept of memes and provides a practical lens through which to analyze the influence and dynamics of these informational patterns in various aspects of human life and culture.

5. Memes exist inside **hosts**, like you and me.
	The fifth point introduces the concept that memes exist inside "hosts," referring to individuals such as yourself and me. This notion aligns with the idea that memes are not standalone entities but are embedded within the cognitive and behavioral frameworks of individuals. The term "hosts" implies that humans serve as carriers or vessels for memes, facilitating their replication and transmission.

6. You and me are hosts, whether you like it or not.
	  The sixth point emphasizes the inescapable nature of individuals, including both you and me, being hosts for memes, regardless of personal preferences. This statement underscores the pervasive influence of memes on human cognition and behavior. The inclusion of "whether you like it or not" suggests that the role of hosts in meme propagation is not a voluntary act; rather, it is an inherent aspect of the interaction between memes and human individuals. This perspective raises questions about agency, autonomy, and the extent to which individuals are aware of or can control the memes that influence them. Exploring the implications of this involuntary hosting and the potential consequences for personal beliefs, behaviors, and cultural dynamics adds depth to the understanding of memes in human society.
	
7. Memes have direct power over hosts, and power over the natural world through us.
	The seventh point introduces the concept that memes have direct power over hosts (individuals) and, in turn, exert power over the natural world through the agency of human hosts. This statement implies a hierarchy of influence, suggesting that memes play a significant role not only in shaping the thoughts and behaviors of individuals but also in extending their impact to the broader environment and the natural world. Understanding the mechanisms through which memes exercise direct power over hosts and examining the cascading effects of this influence on the external world can provide insights into the interconnected relationship between memes, human agency, and the larger ecosystem. This perspective invites exploration into the ethical implications of such influence and the responsibility that comes with the power of memes in shaping both individual lives and the world at large.

Additionally, considering how the mind, as the host, shapes and is shaped by the memes it harbors provides a nuanced perspective on the intricate relationship between human cognition and the influence of replicable information.

	- **Parasitic:** This term suggests a relationship where memes derive benefit at the expense of the hosts. In a parasitic context, memes may exert a negative impact on the well-being or autonomy of individuals.
    
	- **Symbiotic:** This term implies a mutually beneficial relationship between memes and hosts. In a symbiotic context, memes may enhance or support the well-being of individuals, and, in turn, individuals contribute to the propagation or evolution of memes.
    
	- **Benign:** This term suggests a neutral or non-intrusive relationship between memes and hosts. In a benign context, memes may exist without significantly impacting the well-being or behavior of individuals.
    

	Exploring memes through these perspectives involves examining specific instances or types of memes and evaluating their effects on individual hosts. Additionally, considering the ethical implications of different meme-host relationships can provide insights into the complex interplay between cultural information and human well-being.
